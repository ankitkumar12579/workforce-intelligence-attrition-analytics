# workforce-intelligence-attrition-analytics
Business Problem:
Organizations face challenges in retaining employees, leading to increased recruitment costs and productivity loss. This project analyzes workforce data to identify attrition drivers, high-risk employee segments, and actionable retention opportunities.

Dataset Overview:
Total Employees: 1,470
Departments: 3
Job Roles: 9

Key Findings:
Overall attrition rate: 16.12%
Overtime employees showed 30.53% attrition compared to 10.44% for non-overtime employees.
Sales Representatives experienced the highest attrition rate (39.76%).
Employees leaving earned 30% less on average than retained employees.
Poor work-life balance was strongly associated with employee turnover.

Business Recommendations:
Reduce excessive overtime in high-turnover teams.
Improve work-life balance initiatives.
Review compensation strategies for lower salary bands.
Focus retention efforts on Sales Representatives and younger employees.
